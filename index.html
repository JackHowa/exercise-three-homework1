Frederick Douglass graduation rise proportionate to black students

Across the country, more minority students are graduating from high school. According to the Department of Education, 3.7 percent more students who identified as "black" graduated last year. One school in town has a higher percentage of black students (54%) than any other high school in the district. Frederick Douglass High School has seen their school's graduation rates (in grey) nearly double since 2011. Shown in black, you can see the rise in minority graduation rates has been proportionate to Frederick Douglass's higher graduation rate since 2011.

By Jack Howard
Published Feb. 23, 2016
Source: Missouri Dept. of Elementary and Secondary Education, Department of Education
